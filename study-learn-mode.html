Global Alzheimer's Biomarker Validation Consortium

🏥 12 participating sites
🌍 Multi-continental
📅 Started January 2024
👥 5,000 participants
🟢 Currently recruiting

Explore this comprehensive multi-site study validating novel Alzheimer's disease biomarkers across diverse populations. Learn about the scientific methodology, biomarker targets, and collaborative protocols being used to advance AD diagnostics.

Study Objectives

Primary Goal: Validate plasma-based biomarkers for Alzheimer's disease across diverse global populations to improve early diagnosis and monitoring.

  • Validate p-tau217, MTBR-tau243, and GFAP as plasma biomarkers
  • Assess biomarker performance across different ethnicities and populations
  • Establish standardized protocols for multi-site implementation
  • Generate regulatory-quality data for clinical use

Study Design

Participant Population

5,000 participants across the Alzheimer's disease continuum:

  • Cognitively Normal (CN): 1,500 participants
  • Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI): 2,000 participants
  • Alzheimer's Disease Dementia: 1,500 participants

Study Duration

36 months total: 24 months recruitment + 12 months follow-up

Expected Outcomes

  • Validated cutoff values for plasma biomarkers across populations
  • Harmonized protocols for multi-site biomarker testing
  • Regulatory submission package for clinical implementation
  • Published guidelines for AD biomarker testing

Target Biomarkers

This study focuses on three key plasma biomarkers that have shown promise in Alzheimer's disease research:

p-tau217

Function: Phosphorylated tau protein at threonine 217

Significance: Early indicator of tau pathology, correlates with amyloid PET

Expected Performance: >90% accuracy for AD vs controls

MTBR-tau243

Function: Microtubule-binding region tau fragment

Significance: Reflects tau aggregation and neuronal damage

Expected Performance: 85-90% accuracy for disease staging

GFAP

Function: Glial fibrillary acidic protein

Significance: Marker of astrocyte activation and neuroinflammation

Expected Performance: 80-85% accuracy as supportive marker

Why These Biomarkers?

These biomarkers were selected based on extensive preliminary studies showing high correlation with brain pathology and clinical symptoms of Alzheimer's disease.

Scientific Rationale

  • p-tau217: Shows earliest changes in AD pathology, detectable 15+ years before symptoms
  • MTBR-tau243: Provides information about active neurodegeneration
  • GFAP: Indicates inflammatory processes associated with AD progression

Standardized Protocols

All participating sites follow identical protocols to ensure data comparability and reproducibility.

Sample Collection Protocol

  1. Pre-collection: 12-hour fasting, medication review, clinical assessment
  2. Blood Draw: 20mL blood collected in EDTA tubes using standardized procedure
  3. Processing: Samples processed within 2 hours, plasma separated by centrifugation
  4. Storage: Plasma aliquoted and stored at -80°C within 4 hours
  5. Shipping: Samples shipped on dry ice to central laboratory quarterly

Analytical Protocol

Platform: Alamar Biosciences Argo HT

  • Ultra-sensitive digital immunoassay technology
  • Single-molecule detection capabilities
  • Automated sample processing
  • Built-in quality controls

Quality Assurance: Every batch includes positive controls, negative controls, and reference standards. Inter-laboratory comparison samples are analyzed monthly.

Data Management

  • Centralized database with role-based access
  • Real-time data monitoring and quality checks
  • Automated statistical analysis pipelines
  • HIPAA-compliant data storage and transmission
